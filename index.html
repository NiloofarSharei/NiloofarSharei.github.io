About Me

Niloofar (Neal) Sharei

I am a fourth year Ph.D. candidate in physics and astronomy at the University of California, Riverside. I work with Professor Brian Siana and Dr. Alexander de la Vega on the formation and evolution of galaxies in the early universe, with a focus on star forming clumps in galaxies. Using imaging and spectroscopy from HST and JWST, I study how these clumps contribute to galaxy growth and how their properties change across cosmic time.

I am passionate about teaching and science communication, and I enjoy helping students build their first connections with astronomy.

Outside of research, I love hiking, classical and jazz music, and reading world literature.

Research

Origin and fate of giant star-forming Clumps.

Star forming clumps in galaxies (0.5 ≤ z ≤ 3)

In my first project I studied how star forming clumps contribute to galaxy growth, and how inferred clump ages and dust attenuation depend on SED fitting assumptions. Using HST data, I revisited clumps age gradinets with comparison to to the diffuse host galaxy component to test for radial trends and constraints on clump formation and survival.

Teaching

I have been a Teaching Assistant at UCR since Winter 2023, leading laboratory and discussion sections for introductory physics courses taken by engineering, life science, and pre-medical students. My teaching spans classical mechanics, electromagnetism, thermodynamics, and general physics discussion courses.

  • Classical Mechanics Laboratory (PHYS 02LA)
    Taught in Spring 2023, Spring 2024, and Spring 2025. Covered kinematics, Newton‚Äôs laws, energy, momentum, and experimental data analysis.
  • Electromagnetism Laboratory (PHYS 02LB / 02LC)
    Taught in Winter 2023, Fall 2023, Winter 2024, Fall 2024, and Winter 2025. Topics included electric fields, circuits, magnetic fields, induction, and error propagation.
